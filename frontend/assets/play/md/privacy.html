Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2 July 2026 · Applies to: md — the Markdown editor published by nettrash on Google Play (me.nettrash.md), and the same upload-key-signed APK side-loaded from nettrash.me/play/md/md-latest.apk.

This policy is versioned alongside the app's source code; the most recent commit on master is authoritative.

TL;DR

md does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any data. It contains no analytics, no advertising SDKs, no third-party trackers, and no remote servers operated by us. The documents you open and edit stay where you put them — on your device or in whatever storage location you pick — and the app makes no network connections of its own.

If that already answers your question, you don't need to read the rest.

What we collect

Nothing. md has no account to create, no email to register, and no telemetry pinging home from the app. There are no servers in this picture, and the app opens no network connections of its own — it does not even request the INTERNET permission, so its process cannot open a socket.

Your documents

md is a document editor. The files you open, create and save are handled entirely by Android's Storage Access Framework (the system document architecture): you pick each file or folder through the system document picker, and md is granted access only to what you explicitly choose. Your documents are stored wherever you put them — in on-device storage, or in whatever provider you select in the picker (Google Drive, your device's Downloads, an SD card, and so on). We never see them.

If you store a document in a cloud provider through the picker, it syncs through your account with that provider, under their privacy terms, not ours. We have no access to your documents under any circumstance.

Data stored on your device

md keeps no app-private user data — no database, no settings file, no cache of your content. It stores no accounts and no history. Aside from the documents you save (which live in the locations you choose through the picker, not inside the app), the only thing md retains is transient UI state such as the current Edit / Split / Preview layout, held in the app's saved instance state. None of it leaves your device, and none of it contains personal information.

The app manifest leaves Android's Auto Backup at its default (allowBackup="true"). Because md keeps no app-private user data, Auto Backup has nothing app-specific of consequence to capture; whatever the system does back up goes to your own Google account, encrypted, and never to us.

Permissions md asks for

None. md declares no Android permissions at all:

Permission Requested?
INTERNET / network access No. The app makes no network calls; printing renders the document in an offscreen WebView from local HTML the app builds itself — nothing is loaded from the network.
Storage (READ_/WRITE_EXTERNAL_STORAGE, READ_MEDIA_*) No. File access is mediated entirely by the Storage Access Framework, which grants access to the specific files you pick without any storage permission.
Camera, microphone, contacts, location, calendar No — never requested.

Documents are opened three ways, all of them system-mediated: the in-app Open / Save pickers (OpenDocument / CreateDocument, with the access you grant persisted so md can reopen that file); a Markdown or text file handed to md from a file manager or another app (ACTION_VIEW, "Open with md"); and text shared into md to start a new document (ACTION_SEND). In every case md only ever receives the item you chose.

Third-party services

None operating off the device. md is built entirely on Google's first-party Jetpack / Compose libraries with a hand-written Markdown parser and no third-party SDKs. There are no analytics tools (Firebase, Mixpanel, Sentry, etc.), no advertising SDKs (AdMob, Meta, AppLovin, etc.), no crash reporters that phone home, and no attribution providers.

The Google Advertising ID is not requested, accessed, or used. md declares this on its Google Play Data Safety form.

Tracking

md does not "track" you in any sense Google Play recognises in its Data Safety taxonomy: it does not link any data collected in the app with data from other apps, websites, or offline sources to build a user profile, and it does not share any data with data brokers. md is therefore declared with Data not collected and Data not shared on its Google Play Data Safety form.

Children's privacy

md is rated for general audiences (Everyone / PEGI 3) and is suitable for all ages. Because md collects no data at all, it collects no data from children. md is a general-purpose utility, not directed at children under 13.

International data transfers

Because md does not transmit personal data anywhere, there are no cross-border transfers to disclose under the GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA, LGPD, or similar regimes.

Your rights

Because we hold no data about you:

  • There is no record to access under GDPR Article 15 / CCPA "right to know".
  • There is no record to delete under GDPR Article 17 / CCPA "right to delete" (your documents are yours alone — they live where you saved them, and uninstalling md removes the app entirely).
  • There is no record to correct under GDPR Article 16.
  • There is nothing being sold or shared under CCPA / CPRA, so no opt-out is required.

If you'd like confirmation in writing that we hold no data about you, email the address below and we will reply.

Changes to this policy

If a future version of md ever changes any of the above — adds analytics, integrates a third-party SDK, transmits data off-device, or requests new permissions — this document will be updated in the same release and the Effective date at the top will be bumped. The full history is visible in the project's git log on GitHub: github.com/nettrash/md.Android.
