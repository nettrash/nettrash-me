Geo — Support

Applies to: Geo — the iOS app (with Apple Watch companion) published by nettrash on the App Store.

Geo is a hiking instrument: live altitude, atmospheric pressure, and an augmented-reality view that names the mountains around you. This page covers the most common questions; if you don't find the answer, the email link at the bottom reaches a human.

Get in touch

The fastest way to reach the developer is email: nettrash@nettrash.me. Please include your iPhone model and iOS version — it makes sensor and AR issues much easier to reproduce.

Frequently asked questions

Why is the altitude reading 0?

Geo reads altitude from the iPhone's barometric pressure sensor (CMAltimeter), which is gated by the system's Motion & Fitness permission. Open Settings → Privacy & Security → Motion & Fitness and make sure Geo is enabled. iPhone models without a barometric sensor (e.g. the original iPhone SE) can't show barometric altitude; the GPS altitude reading is still shown when Location is granted.

Why are no peaks shown in the AR view?

Peaks are looked up from Apple Maps and OpenStreetMap, both of which need network access. The AR view also assumes the camera has line of sight to the actual sky — pointing it at an interior wall will show the camera feed and a "Scanning" indicator but no peak labels. If you're indoors, point the camera out a window or step outside; if your area genuinely has no catalogued peaks within the 5 km search radius, the view shows the horizon line by itself.

Why is the horizon line cyan instead of green?

The green skyline is drawn from real terrain elevation data fetched from a public elevation database. When that database is briefly unreachable (slow network, the service is down), Geo falls back to a geometric sea-level horizon, drawn in cyan. Both track your camera correctly; the difference is how realistic the line is over distant mountains.

Where is my altitude history stored, and how do I delete it?

On your devices, and in your private iCloud. Altitude, pressure, and route history sync across iPhone and Apple Watch via CloudKit. Nothing is uploaded to any server the developer runs; the developer has no access to your data. Full details are in the privacy policy.

To delete the history: remove Geo from your iPhone and clear the Geo entry from Settings → [your name] → iCloud → Manage Account Storage.

My Apple Watch complications stopped updating.

Complications refresh on the watch's own schedule and after a new barometer reading is recorded. Open the Geo Watch app once and let it run for a few seconds; the complication will catch up at the next refresh. If it's still stuck, remove the complication from the watch face and re-add it.

The widget shows stale altitude.

iPhone widgets refresh on the system's schedule (typically every couple of minutes) and Geo additionally requests an immediate refresh whenever it captures a new barometer reading. If you've just opened the app for the first time, give the widget a minute to catch up. To force a refresh: long-press the widget, choose Edit Widget, and re-confirm.

I denied a permission. How do I re-enable it?

Geo shows a banner across the top of the tab view explaining what's missing and offering a one-tap shortcut to Settings. Granting the permission there clears the banner immediately on return.

Reporting bugs

If something looks wrong, please describe what you did, what you expected, and what actually happened. A screenshot or screen recording helps a lot. Send it to nettrash@nettrash.me; we'll respond as soon as we can.
