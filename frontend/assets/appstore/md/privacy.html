Privacy Policy

Last updated: 30 June 2026 · Applies to: md — the Markdown editor published by nettrash on the App Store. The same product runs on iPhone and iPad (iOS / iPadOS) and on Mac (a native macOS app). This policy is versioned alongside the app's source code; the most recent commit on main is authoritative.

TL;DR

md does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any data. It contains no analytics, no advertising SDKs, no third-party trackers, and no servers operated by us. The documents you open and edit stay where you put them — on your device or Mac, in Files / Finder, or in your own iCloud Drive. The app makes no network connections of its own.

If that already answers your question, you don't need to read the rest.

What we collect

Nothing. md has no account to create, no email to register, and no telemetry pinging home from the app. There are no servers in this picture and the app opens no network connections of its own.

Your documents

md is a document editor. The files you open, create and save are handled entirely by Apple's system document architecture (DocumentGroup / FileDocument) and are stored wherever you choose — locally on your device or Mac, in the Files app / Finder, or in iCloud Drive. We never see them.

If you store a document in iCloud Drive, it syncs through your Apple Account under Apple's privacy terms, not ours. We have no access to your documents under any circumstance.

The app stores one small preference on-device — your last-used Edit / Split / Preview layout, per window — via the standard system preferences store. It never leaves your device and contains no personal information.

Permissions

md requests no special permissions — no camera, microphone, contacts, location, photo-library, or network access. File access is mediated entirely by the system:

Platform How files are accessed
iPhone / iPad Files are chosen through the system document browser. The app is sandboxed and only ever sees the documents you explicitly open or create.
Mac The app runs in the macOS App Sandbox and requests only user-selected file access (com.apple.security.files.user-selected.read-write): it can read and write the documents you open or save, and nothing else.

Data the App Store privacy label declares

md's App Store privacy label is "Data Not Collected" across the board — no data of any category is collected, linked to you, or used for tracking:

Data type Collected?
Contact info, identifiers, usage data, diagnostics, location, content you createNo — none of it leaves your device except where you place a file in your own iCloud Drive.
Analytics / advertising / trackingNone. The app contains no such SDKs.

Children's privacy

Because md collects no data at all, it collects no data from children.

Changes to this policy

Any change is committed to this file in the app's public source repository, so the history is auditable.
